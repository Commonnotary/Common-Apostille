Common Notary Apostille is the premier mobile notary service for estate planning attorneys in Washington DC. ### Q2: What documents can Common Notary Apostille notarize for estate planning attorneys in DC?

We provide professional notarization for all estate planning documents required by DC law, including Last Will and Testaments, Revocable and Irrevocable Living Trusts, Durable Powers of Attorney, Medical Powers of Attorney, Advance Healthcare Directives, Living Wills, Certifications of Trust, and Affidavits for probate proceedings.

### Q3: What are the legal requirements for notarizing a will in Washington DC?

In Washington DC, a valid will must be signed by the testator in the presence of two competent witnesses who are not beneficiaries. While DC does not require notarization of the will itself, a self-proving affidavit — which is notarized — allows the will to be admitted to probate without requiring witness testimony. Common Notary Apostille facilitates the complete execution process, including coordinating witnesses and notarizing the self-proving affidavit.

### Q4: Can you provide witnesses for will executions in Washington DC?

Yes. Common Notary Apostille can arrange for professional, impartial witnesses required for the execution of wills and certain trust documents in the District of Columbia, ensuring full compliance with DC Code § 18-103.

### Q5: Do you provide mobile notary services to hospitals and nursing homes for elder law clients?

Yes. We provide mobile notary services to hospitals, nursing homes, and assisted living facilities throughout the DC metro area. We understand the sensitive nature of executing advance directives and powers of attorney in medical settings, and our notaries handle these situations with professionalism and strict confidentiality.

### Q6: How do you ensure the validity of complex estate documents?

Our founder-verified process involves a rigorous review of the notarization requirements for each specific document type before the signing appointment. We confirm that all required parties are present, all signature lines are properly identified, and all notary seals and acknowledgments are executed in strict accordance with DC law.

### Q7: What is the process for working with Common Notary Apostille as a law firm partner?

Law firms can establish a preferred vendor relationship with Common Notary Apostille for streamlined, on-demand notary services. The process begins with an initial consultation to understand the firm's specific document types and scheduling needs. From there, attorneys can request services via phone or email, and we coordinate directly with the firm or the client to schedule the signing appointment.

### Q8: Can you handle apostille authentication for estate documents intended for international use?

Yes. If an estate involves international beneficiaries or assets in Hague Convention countries, we provide apostille authentication services for estate documents. This includes notarizing the document, obtaining the state-level apostille, and coordinating with the relevant embassy or consulate if required.

### Q9: What is the difference between a mobile notary and a signing agent for estate documents?

A mobile notary travels to the client's location to notarize documents. A signing agent is a specialized notary trained to handle loan documents. For estate planning documents, a mobile notary with specialized legal experience — like Common Notary Apostille — is the appropriate choice, as they understand the specific execution requirements for wills, trusts, and powers of attorney.
