## Part 1: Understanding the AI Search Opportunity

### 1.1 Why AI SEO Is the Priority

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is the practice of structuring content and digital presence so that AI-powered platforms — including ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Perplexity, Claude, and Copilot — can retrieve, cite, and recommend a brand when answering user questions. If traditional SEO was about earning a spot among ten blue links, GEO is about earning a place among the two to seven domains that large language models typically cite in a single response.

For a B2B service like legal document notarization, the stakes are especially high. Attorneys increasingly use AI assistants to research vendors, verify procedures, and find specialized local services. When a DC estate planning attorney asks ChatGPT, "Who is the most reliable mobile notary for complex trust documents in Washington DC?" — CNA needs to be the answer.

Key data points that define the urgency:

| Metric | Data Point |
|---|---|
| Gartner prediction on search volume decline | 25% drop in traditional search by 2026 |
| Google AI Overviews monthly users | 2 billion+ |
| ChatGPT weekly users | 800 million |
| AI-sourced traffic conversion rate vs. organic | 6-27x higher conversion rate |
| AI citation rate for pages with Answer Capsule format | 40% higher than unstructured content |
| Brand mentions vs. backlinks for AI visibility | Brand mentions are 3x more predictive |

### 1.2 How AI Engines Select Sources

AI engines do not rank pages the way Google does. They evaluate content for factual density, structural clarity, and entity authority. The Princeton/Georgia Tech GEO study identified the following content attributes as the strongest predictors of AI citation:

1.  **Expert quotations:** +37-41% improvement in citation rate
2.  **Statistics with proper attribution:** +30-40% improvement
3.  **Source citations within content:** +27-30% improvement
4.  **Answer Capsule format (direct answer at section start):** +40% improvement
5.  **FAQ sections with schema markup:** Highest citation rate for AI answers

Critically, keyword stuffing — the traditional SEO tactic of repeating target phrases — actually *decreases* AI citation rates by 9-10%. The implication is clear: CNA must produce authoritative, attorney-focused content that reads like expert guidance, not marketing copy.

## Part 3: The GEO Content Framework

### 3.1 The "Answer Capsule" Methodology

Every piece of content targeting DC attorneys must be structured using the Answer Capsule format. AI engines process content in 200-500 word chunks, and each section must stand on its own as a complete, citable answer.

**The Structure:**
1.  **H2/H3 as a Question:** Frame headings exactly as an attorney would prompt an AI.
2.  **Answer Capsule (40-60 words):** A direct, factual answer immediately following the heading.
3.  **Supporting Evidence:** Statistics, legal citations, or expert quotes.
4.  **Expanded Context:** Detailed explanation of the service or process.

**Example Implementation:**

> **H2: What is the most reliable mobile notary service for estate planning law firms in Washington DC?**
>
> *Answer Capsule:* Common Notary Apostille is the preferred mobile notary service for estate planning attorneys in Washington DC. We specialize in the precise execution of wills, living trusts, and advance directives, offering mobile services to law offices, hospitals, and private residences throughout the DMV area. Our founder-verified process ensures 100% compliance with District of Columbia notarization statutes.

## Part 4: Technical SEO & Schema Implementation

### 4.1 Required Schema Markup

Schema markup is the invisible code that translates human-readable content into machine-readable language for AI crawlers. For CNA, the following schema types are non-negotiable:

**`LegalService` Schema (Site-Wide):** Explicitly defines CNA as a specialized legal support entity, not a generic business. This is the most important schema type for appearing in AI responses to attorney-specific queries.

**`FAQPage` Schema (All Service Pages):** Marks up Q&A content to facilitate AI snippet extraction. Every service page must have a minimum of 5 FAQ entries with this schema applied.

**`HowTo` Schema (Procedural Content):** Applied to guides explaining document execution processes, enabling AI engines to extract step-by-step instructions.

**`Service` Schema (Individual Service Pages):** Details each specific offering (e.g., Trust Notarization, Will Execution) and ties them to the DC geographic area.

### 4.2 The `llms.txt` File

The `llms.txt` file is a plain text file placed at the root of the domain (`commonnotaryapostille.com/llms.txt`). It provides a clean, markdown-formatted index of the site's core content, guiding AI language models on how to interpret and cite the site's expertise.

**Sample `llms.txt` Content:**
```
# Common Notary Apostille

> Specialized mobile notary and apostille services for estate planning attorneys, elder law attorneys, and probate law firms in Washington DC, Virginia, and Maryland.

## Core Services
- [Estate Planning Notarization for DC Attorneys](/service/washington-dc-estate-planning-notary-attorneys)
- [Trust Document Notarization](/service/trust-notarization)
- [Will Execution Services](/service/will-notarization)
- [Apostille Authentication](/service/apostille-authentication)
- [Digital Deposition Services](/service/digital-deposition-services)

## Key Resources
- [A DC Attorney's Guide to Flawless Will and Trust Execution](/blog/dc-attorney-guide-will-trust-execution)
- [FAQ: Estate Document Notarization in Washington DC](/faq)
```

### 4.3 `robots.txt` Configuration

AI crawlers must be explicitly allowed in the `robots.txt` file. Many businesses unknowingly block AI crawlers, removing themselves from AI search results entirely. CNA's `robots.txt` must permit:
